Giraffe

The Giraffe (Giraffa camelopardalis meaning fast walking camel leopard) is an African even-toed ungulate mammal, the tallest of all land-living animal species.

Scientific Name: Giraffa Camelopardalis.
Kingdom: Animalia.
Phylum: Chordata.
Class: Mammalia.

A giraffe's neck is too short to reach the ground. As a result, it has to awkwardly spread its front legs or kneel to reach the ground for a drink of water.
